### 3.3 Approximate location (only with your consent)

If you opt in via the "Share location" toggle in your profile settings, the Service uses your device's GPS to broadcast your current latitude/longitude to other members of your active Trip every time the location updates. This data is stored on your user document as `lastKnownLocation` and is **only** read by other members of the same Trip while location-sharing is enabled. You can revoke this at any time in profile settings or in your operating-system permissions.

## 5. With whom we share your data

We share personal data only with the categories of recipients described below. We **do not** sell personal data, and we **do not** share it with advertisers or data brokers.

### 5.1 Other Trip members

Data you add to a Trip — including your name, avatar, photos, expenses, votes, location (if you opted in), and any tags you placed on photos or activities — is visible to other members of that Trip. Members can also see your username and country. Phone number is shown to Trip members only if you have set the `sharePhoneNumber` flag to true.

When you leave a Trip, your historical contributions remain in the Trip's record (because removing them would corrupt other members' data — for example, an expense you paid still affects the balance calculations). Your name, avatar, and content tags will continue to be displayed to remaining Trip members.

## 7. How long we keep your data (retention)

| Data category | Retention period |
|---|---|
| Account identity (name, email, hashed password) | While your account is active. Deleted within **30 days** of your account-deletion request, except where law requires longer retention. |
| Public profile (avatar, country, bucketlist, etc.) | Same as account identity |
| Phone number (private subcollection) | Same as account identity. Deleted immediately if you remove it from your profile. |
| Trip membership records | While you are a member. After leaving, your contributions remain in the Trip but are no longer linked to an active profile (see §5.1). |
| Expense and payment records | While the underlying Trip exists. Trip creators may delete a Trip; afterwards the records are deleted within 30 days from backup retention. |
| Receipt images | Kept for as long as the related expense exists in a Trip. |
| Gallery photos | Kept for as long as the Trip exists or until you (the uploader) delete the photo. |
| Logs (IP, user-agent, error stacks) | Up to **12 months**, after which they are deleted or anonymised. |
| Email-verification + sign-in audit trail | Up to **12 months** for security investigation. |
| Records required for legal/tax compliance | As long as required by applicable law (typically up to **7 years** for accounting records under Swedish bookkeeping law). |
| Backups | Encrypted backups of Firestore are retained for up to **35 days** after which they roll off automatically. |

When the retention period ends, data is either deleted or rendered irreversibly anonymous so that the person can no longer be identified.

## 8. Your rights under GDPR

If you are an individual in the EU/EEA, you have the rights below. You can exercise any of them by contacting us at [privacy@tripmates.app](mailto:privacy@tripmates.app), or by using the self-service tools described.

| Right | What it means | How to use it |
|---|---|---|
| **Access** (Art. 15) | Get a copy of the personal data we hold about you. | Profile → Settings → "Download my data" generates a JSON export immediately. Or email us. |
| **Rectification** (Art. 16) | Correct inaccurate data. | Edit your profile in-app. For data you can't edit yourself, email us. |
| **Erasure** (Art. 17, "right to be forgotten") | Have us delete your personal data. | Profile → Settings → Danger zone → "Delete my account". The deletion is processed by a Cloud Function within seconds and propagates to backups within 35 days. |
| **Restriction of processing** (Art. 18) | Pause processing of your data while a dispute or accuracy check is resolved. | Email us. |
| **Data portability** (Art. 20) | Receive your data in a structured, commonly-used, machine-readable format and transmit it to another controller. | The "Download my data" export is structured JSON, which satisfies portability. |
| **Object** (Art. 21) | Object to processing based on legitimate interest. | Email us with reasons. |
| **Withdraw consent** (Art. 7(3)) | Withdraw consent at any time for any processing based on Art. 6(1)(a). Withdrawal does not affect processing that already happened. | Toggle the relevant setting in-app (e.g. share-location), or email us. |
| **Automated decision-making** (Art. 22) | Be informed about / object to solely-automated decisions with legal or similarly significant effects. | Not applicable — we don't use such decisions. |

We respond to requests **within 30 days** (extendable by another two months for complex requests, with notice).

You can also lodge a complaint with a supervisory authority. In Sweden the authority is **Integritetsskyddsmyndigheten (IMY)** — [imy.se](https://www.imy.se), Box 8114, 104 20 Stockholm. You can complain in any EU/EEA member state where you live, where you work, or where you believe the violation occurred.

## 9. Security

We protect your data with appropriate technical and organisational measures, including:

- **Encryption in transit** for all client–server traffic (HTTPS / TLS 1.2+).
- **Encryption at rest** of Firestore documents, Cloud Storage objects, and database backups, managed by Google.
- **Authentication via Firebase Authentication** with industry-standard password hashing.
- **Granular database access rules** (Firestore security rules) that restrict each user to only their own and shared-trip data, with separate per-trip admin / member tiers and field-level write locks on sensitive operations such as payments and bingo squares.
- **Server-side server-only role grants** — admin role is granted only by a server process; client writes attempting to escalate are rejected.
- **Storage rules requiring authentication** for all media reads.
- **Regular dependency scanning** through GitHub's automated security alerts.
- **Continuous integration** that runs typechecks, lint, and security-rules tests on every change.

Despite these measures, no system can guarantee 100% security. If we become aware of a personal data breach affecting your data, we will notify the supervisory authority within 72 hours and notify you without undue delay where the breach is likely to result in a high risk to your rights and freedoms (Art. 33–34 GDPR).
